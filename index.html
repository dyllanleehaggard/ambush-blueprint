Part VII

INFRASTRUCTURE & VENUE STRATEGY

The single biggest constraint on our growth — and a concrete path to solving it.

Operational
No daily access · No control over training conflicts · Limited game-day authority · Athletic trainer cramped in shared space with no dedicated setup
Revenue
Cannot monetize concessions · No parking revenue · No premium inventory control · No off-season event hosting — every revenue ceiling hits the same wall
Strategic
Growth gated by someone else's calendar · No permanent branding possible · Every year is a lease renewal conversation · Long-term stability is not guaranteed
Two of our highest-potential revenue streams — concessions and parking — are blocked by the venue arrangement. This is not a marketing problem. It is an infrastructure problem. Solving venue dependency solves the revenue ceiling.

The Model — Union Omaha & Reno 1868

Union Omaha
Two-time USL League One champion. Shared venue (baseball stadium), lean front office, culture-first. Planning a downtown stadium/mixed-use district for 2028 — city-backed deal, not club capital. Philosophy: out-operate, not out-spend.
Reno 1868 FC
$350M mixed-use district anchored by a soccer stadium. Holding company structure, phased development, city partnership model. Neither wrote a $50M check. The club made the civic case — the city invested because the math worked for the city.
We are not asking for a stadium. We are making the case that a soccer club is a civic asset — and letting the city invest.

The Civic Pitch Framework

"The St. Louis Ambush is a 13-year professional sports franchise — the longest-operating professional soccer club in St. Louis. A soccer-anchored mixed-use district would activate underused property, generate tax revenue, drive tourism, and serve as a year-round civic gathering space. The World Cup in 2026, the Olympics in 2028, and the Women's World Cup in 2031 have created a generational window to build lasting infrastructure around that momentum. We are requesting a ground lease, TIF financing, or infrastructure partnership. Not a cash grant."
